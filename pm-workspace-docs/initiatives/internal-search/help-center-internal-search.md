# Internal Search: Your AI-Powered Meeting Intelligence Assistant

> **Feature Status:** Beta  
> **Available to:** All workspace users with Internal Search enabled

---

## What is Internal Search?

Internal Search lets you ask questions about your customer conversations in plain English. Instead of manually hunting through meeting recordings, transcripts, and notes, simply ask what you need to know‚Äîand AskElephant finds it for you.

**Think of it as Google for your customer conversations**, but smarter. It understands what you mean, not just the exact words you type.

---

## How to Enable Internal Search

1. Open any chat in AskElephant
2. Click the **Tools** button (puzzle piece icon) in the chat composer
3. Find **"Internal Search"** with the purple database icon
4. Toggle it **ON**

Once enabled, AskElephant will automatically use Internal Search when your questions are about your meeting history, customers, or team.

---

## What Can You Ask?

Internal Search can find information across:

| Data Type | Examples |
|-----------|----------|
| **Meetings** | Calls, recordings, calendar events |
| **Companies** | Customers, prospects, partners, accounts |
| **Contacts** | People you've met with |
| **Team Members** | Your colleagues and their activity |
| **Signals** | Extracted insights (objections, sentiment, action items) |
| **Workflow Outputs** | AI-generated summaries and notes |

## Pro Tips for Better Results

### 1. Be Specific About Time
Instead of broad searches, add time context:
- ‚ùå "Show me Acme meetings"
- ‚úÖ "Show me Acme meetings from the last 2 weeks"

### 2. Use Natural Language
You don't need special syntax. Ask like you're asking a colleague:
- ‚ùå "query: acme AND objection"  
- ‚úÖ "What objections did Acme raise in our recent calls?"

### 3. Search by Meaning, Not Just Words
Internal Search uses semantic search‚Äîit understands concepts:
- Searching for "pricing concerns" will also find discussions about "cost", "budget", "too expensive", etc.

### 4. Combine Filters Naturally
You can mix entity types and time ranges:
- "Show me Sarah's meetings with enterprise accounts last month where security was discussed"

### 5. Follow Up for Details
Start broad, then drill down:
1. "Show me recent meetings with CloudCorp"
2. "What were the action items from the January 10th call?"
3. "Read me the part where they discussed timeline"

## What Internal Search Cannot Do

To set proper expectations:

| Cannot Do | Use This Instead |
|-----------|------------------|
| Access CRM deal data directly | Enable CRM tools in addition to Internal Search |
| Generate new analysis on the fly | Sets up workflows to extract insights automatically |
| Find meetings that weren't recorded | Ensure consistent recording coverage |
| Search email content | Use email integrations separately |

## FAQ

### Q: Does it search the actual transcript or just summaries?

**Both!** Internal Search can:
- Search semantically across transcript content
- Find workflow-generated summaries and notes
- Look up extracted signals and data points

### Q: How far back can I search?

As far back as you have data. Internal Search queries your entire workspace history.

### Q: Can I search for meetings I wasn't on?

Yes, if you have workspace access. Internal Search respects your permissions‚Äîyou'll see meetings you have access to view.

### Q: Why is it marked "Beta"?

We're actively improving the feature based on user feedback. Beta means:
- The feature is functional and useful
- We're still enhancing capabilities
- We'd love your feedback on what works and what could be better

### Q: What if I can't find what I'm looking for?

Try these steps:
1. Broaden your time range
2. Use different phrasing (semantic search helps, but alternative terms can too)
3. Check if the meeting was recorded
4. Make sure Internal Search is enabled in your tools

Still stuck? Reach out to support‚Äîwe want to know when searches don't work as expected.
